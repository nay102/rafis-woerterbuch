Daily Habits That Make a Difference

Learning German becomes easier when you develop small, consistent study habits.

01

Study Every Day

Even 20–30 minutes of daily practice is more effective than long study sessions once a week.

02

Speak Out Loud

Reading and repeating sentences aloud will improve both pronunciation and confidence.

03

Review Regularly

Revisiting vocabulary and grammar helps move knowledge into your long-term memory.

04

Use German Daily

Watch videos, listen to music, read simple articles and think in German whenever possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have Questions?

Here are some common questions from German learners.

Which level should I start with?

If you are completely new to German, start with A1. If you already know the basics, choose the level that matches your current knowledge.

Is this course suitable for Goethe exams?

Yes. The learning path follows the CEFR levels (A1–B2) and is designed to help learners prepare for Goethe examinations.

Will new lessons be added?

Absolutely. Rafis Sprachwelt will continue to grow with new lessons, vocabulary, grammar topics, exercises and additional learning resources.
